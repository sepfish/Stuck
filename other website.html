Stuck

by: Kayla Brewer

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I made this game because I like small puzzle games. I've looked for a lot of "escape the room" games but haven't found many of them (that I would trust enough to play). So, I decided to make one myself.

2. What I Learned

I found that the game turned out to take a while to play with no hints, so I had to provide a couple of hints to make sure the testing didn't take forever. After that, I made adjustments so it was a little easier to figure out some things. I also added sound effects because of feedback.

3. Architecture of My Game

In this game, you control a character that can interact with different objects placed in the rooms. You can move left and right as long as you are walking on the floor and up and down as long as you are on a ladder. You can "check" objects by standing in front of them and pressing "enter". Some objects will have different events if you check them.

4. Technical Implementation

The Character that you control and the interactObjects that you interact with both stem from a base gameObject class. The ObjectManager is in charge of initializing interactObjects, drawing rooms, and contains the massive, 192-line checking method. The GamePanel is in charge of everything else. I am most proud of implementing the checking method. This method allows the player to check if there are clues inside objects and add things into their inventory. A lot of the objects in the game have their own specific events for when they are checked, and some have different events for the number of times you check them.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of this project was implementing the checking method and debugging everything. As mentioned above, the checking method was very complicated and I had to do a lot of debugging purely on this method. Debugging the whole project was also challenging since a lot of the methods were all intertwined and finding the source of a problem could take a long time.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would like to make the rooms seem more "lively". Although I tried to add a lot of things in the rooms, they still look kind of empty. I also kind of wanted to add dynamic transition screens between rooms, but I kind of gave up on the idea.
